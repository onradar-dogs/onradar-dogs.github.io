Dog Training Tips & Tricks

Expert advice on training, grooming, exercise, and behavior management for dogs of all breeds.

Training Fundamentals

Positive Reinforcement

Reward-based training is the most effective approach for teaching dogs new behaviors. Use treats, praise, and affection to encourage desired behaviors. Avoid harsh corrections, which can create fear and damage your relationship with your dog.

💡 Pro Tip: Use high-value treats (small, soft, and tasty) for training sessions. Reserve extra-special treats for teaching new commands.

Consistency is Key

All family members should use the same commands and rules. Inconsistency confuses dogs and slows learning. Establish clear expectations and stick to them.

Keep Sessions Short

Train for 5-15 minute sessions, multiple times daily if possible. Dogs have shorter attention spans than humans, and longer sessions become counterproductive. Multiple short sessions are more effective than one long session.

Grooming & Coat Care

Brushing Routines

  • Short-coated breeds: Brush 1-2 times weekly
  • Long-coated breeds: Brush 3-5 times weekly
  • Double-coated breeds: Brush 2-3 times weekly, daily during shedding season
  • Curly/wavy coats: Professional grooming every 6-8 weeks plus regular brushing

Regular brushing prevents mats, reduces shedding, and keeps your dog's coat healthy. It's also an excellent bonding opportunity.

Bathing Best Practices

Most dogs need bathing only every 4-8 weeks, unless they're particularly dirty or have skin conditions. Over-bathing can dry out their skin and coat. Use lukewarm water and dog-specific shampoo.

💡 Pro Tip: Brush your dog thoroughly before bathing to remove mats. Mats become harder to remove once wet.

Exercise & Activity

Daily Exercise Guidelines

  • Puppies: Short sessions (5 minutes per month of age), multiple times daily
  • Adult dogs: 30-60 minutes daily, depending on breed energy level
  • Senior dogs: Shorter, gentler activities; multiple short walks preferred

Mental Stimulation

Mental exercise is as important as physical exercise. Provide puzzle toys, training sessions, scent games, and interactive play. A mentally stimulated dog is a well-behaved dog.

Suggestion: Hide treats around the house for your dog to find, rotate toys to keep them interesting, and practice training commands during play.

Behavior Management

Addressing Excessive Barking

Identify the trigger (boredom, anxiety, alert barking). Address the underlying cause rather than just punishing the behavior. Provide adequate exercise, mental stimulation, and training.

Handling Separation Anxiety

  • Start with short absences and gradually increase duration
  • Create a safe, comfortable space for your dog
  • Use calming aids like anxiety wraps or calming music
  • Consult a professional trainer if severe

Managing Jumping

Ignore jumping behavior and reward calm behavior. Don't give attention (even negative attention) when your dog jumps. Only provide attention when they're sitting or standing calmly.

Health & Wellness Tips

Dental Care

Brush your dog's teeth daily if possible, at minimum 3-4 times weekly. Schedule professional cleanings as recommended by your vet. Dental disease is preventable with proper care.

Nail Care

Trim nails every 3-4 weeks or when you hear them clicking on floors. Long nails affect gait and can cause pain. Consider professional grooming for nail care.

Ear Health

Check ears weekly for odor, redness, or excess wax. Dogs with floppy ears need more frequent ear cleaning. Use vet-approved ear cleaners and follow proper technique.

💡 Pro Tip: Many trainers and groomers are excellent resources for personalized advice. Don't hesitate to ask for recommendations during routine visits.

Nutrition Tips

Proper nutrition is the foundation of health. Feed high-quality dog food appropriate for your dog's age and size. Consult your vet about specific dietary needs, especially for large breeds or dogs with health concerns.

Avoid table scraps and human foods, many of which are toxic to dogs (chocolate, grapes, onions, xylitol). Use training treats that are small and low-calorie so you don't exceed daily caloric needs.
